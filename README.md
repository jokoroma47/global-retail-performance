# Global Retail Performance — Revenue, Profit & Market Intelligence

A data analytics project examining four years of global 
retail transactions across 7 markets, 147 countries and 
3 product categories — uncovering the profitability drivers, 
problem areas and growth patterns behind $12.6 million 
in revenue.

---

## Overview

Retail profitability is rarely about revenue alone. 
I built this project to identify where profit is actually 
being made and destroyed — which markets underperform, 
which sub-categories lose money despite strong revenue, 
and how discounting behaviour is systematically eroding 
margins across parts of the business.

The dataset covers 51,290 order rows across 2011 to 2014, 
spanning 25,035 unique orders, 1,590 customers, 10,292 
products and 147 countries.

---

## What I Found

- Revenue grew 90% over 4 years from $2.26M to $4.30M — 
  a 23.9% CAGR — with profit margin holding consistently 
  between 11 and 12% throughout
- Tables is the only loss-making sub-category — $757K 
  revenue but -$64K profit at -8.46% margin. Its average 
  discount of 29% vs the company-wide 14% is the direct 
  cause
- Discounts above 20% destroy profitability entirely. 
  31-50% discounts produce -32.4% margins. 51%+ discounts 
  lose $1.11 for every $1.00 of revenue
- EMEA is a profitability red flag — only 5.45% margin 
  on $806K revenue, driven by the highest average discount 
  rate of any market at 20%
- Canada is the most profitable market at 26.6% — 
  exactly because it applies zero discounts
- The top customer by revenue (Sean Miller at $25K) is 
  actually loss-making at -7.9% margin — high revenue 
  does not equal high profitability
- Technology leads both revenue ($4.74M) and margin (14%) 
  — Furniture trails badly at 6.94% margin despite $4.11M 
  in revenue
- Q4 is consistently the strongest quarter every year 
  without exception — a reliable seasonal pattern
